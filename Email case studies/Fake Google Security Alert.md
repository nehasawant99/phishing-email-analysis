# Case 5 – Fake Google Security Alert

# Objective
Analyse a phishing email impersonating Google with a fake security warning.

<img width="1452" height="802" alt="Screenshot 2026-05-29 at 11 50 29 AM" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/62ba27f8-5bbb-4f18-b45c-2ff8aa972749" />


# Findings
* Claims to be from Google.
* Uses a fake sender domain (google.support).
* Warns about a government-backed attack.
* Contains a link leading to a fake login page.

# Red Flags
* Fake sender domain.
* Fear-based security warning.
* Fake login page.
* The link doesn't point to the official Google website.

# Impact
Victims may enter their Google credentials, leading to account compromise.

# Recommendation
- Check the sender's domain carefully.
- Visit Google directly instead of clicking email links.
- Verify security alerts from your Google Account page.


# Key Learning
- Official Google emails use trusted Google domains.
- Don't trust security alerts without verifying the URL.
- Always inspect links before entering credentials.
